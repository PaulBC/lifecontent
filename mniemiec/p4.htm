Frequency of occurrence | Methuselahs with P2 censuses

Because Mold is the smallest period-4 oscillator, and its rotor can grow on any object with a loaf-like bonding site, most period-4 oscillators are mold-based. In fact, these alone comprise around 94% of all such oscillators up to 25 bits. The next two easily-expandible period-4 oscillators occur at 20 bits: Confused eaters and Eater block frob. Variants of these each comprise around 2.5% of period-4 oscillators up to 25 bits, with less than 1% remaining that do not belong to one of these three categories.

The 2 twelve-bit period 4 oscillators


Mold [5] Mazing [5]

Mold is the smallest oscillator that produces a period 4 diagonal bit spark. Also, mold forms on most still-lifes that have a bonding surface like the long diagonal side of a loaf (from which its name was inspired), so it forms the basis for many larger period 4 oscillators (and many with periods that are multiples of 4, such as period 12).

The 3 eighteen-bit period 4 oscillators


Mono- gram; Initials; JHC [15] Up mold on table [10] Down mold on table [10]

Monogram (whose name is derived from the fact that it resembles the letters JHC, the initials of John Horton Conway, the inventor of Life), while it has no sparks, can be used as a sparker oscillator in some circumstances, because it can delete blinkers.
