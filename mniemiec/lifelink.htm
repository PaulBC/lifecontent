Life programs

These are various programs useful for exploring Life on various platforms:

  • Life32. (by Johan Bontes. 32-bit Windows 95, 98, ME, NT 4, 2000, XP, etc. Open source.)
  • Golly. (by Andrew Trevorrow, Tom Rokicki, et al.; Windows).
  • LifeLib. (by Andrew Trevorrow. Macintosh. Fast, with lots of features. Shareware.)
  • Xlife-3.5. (By Jon Bennett. Freeware. Written in C. Running under Unix with X11 graphics.)
  • Life 1.06. (by Alan Hensel. DOS. Very fast. Shareware.)
  • Mirek's Cellebration. (by Mirek Wojtowicz. Windows. Supports may different cellular automata.)
  • SARCASim. (by George Maydwell. Windows. A more general-purpose cellular automata program. The acronym stands for "Super Animation-Reduction Cellular Automata Simulator".) (NOTE: There are 11 CA Java applet sub-windows on the main window, so these cause many browser confirmation popups. These can be annoying, but they are totally safe.)
  • HLife. (by Tomas Rokicki's. This is now obsolete, as its functionality has been totally subsumed into Golly).
