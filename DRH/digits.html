Patterns which die
------------------

Eric first asked if infinitely many such patterns die.  It's easy to see
that the answer is "yes"; e.g. numbers of the form 1444...444 all die in
9 gens.  Arbitrarily slow death is also possible; e.g. numbers of the form
1125344743766111...111947742, with an odd number of 1's, die by crashing
spaceships:

Still-lifes, oscillators, and spaceships
----------------------------------------

Of course, death isn't the most interesting thing that can happen to
a Life pattern.  Here's a collection of numbers which produce small
still-lifes, oscillators, and spaceships.  In each of these, the named
object is produced by itself.  Most of these were found by observing
that some number produced an uncommon still-life, and then modifying
the initial and final digits to get rid of the debris.

Repeated strings of digits
--------------------------

Long strings of a single digit produce 4 different results:

A string of 1's, 3's, 4's, 6's, 8's, or 9's dies quickly, except at the
ends.  These are useful for large constructions, since they allow us to
connect various components, as long as the components can be embedded
between two strings of dying digits.  For example, the bi-ship
predecessor given earlier is made from an embeddable digit string which
produces a fleet (...11150038111...) and one which produces a LWSS
(...11131041653546111...).  Most of my experiments have been with such
embeddable digit strings.

As noted earlier, a long string of 2's produces two lines of blinkers; so
does a string of 5's.

A string of 0's produces a wick which is stable in the middle but decays
from the ends at 4c/5, leaving behind a lot of debris.  There are at least
5 different fuses of this type, formed, for example, by the numbers
1000..., 2000..., 6000..., 7000..., and 8000... .  (To see these in action
requires too many 0's to include the patterns here.)  Here's a short string
of 0's, stabilized with tubs at the ends; I don't know if there are strings
of digits that can be put at the ends to stabilize the wick:

Creating spaceships and gliders
-------------------------------

The small pattern collection shown earlier was the result of running lots
of numeric patterns and seeing what they produce.  In some cases, the
still-life or oscillator was produced along with some other stuff, and
the ends of the numbers had to be modified to eliminate the debris.  No
doubt a few other things could be produced in this way.  But it's also
possible to build things that don't occur naturally.
